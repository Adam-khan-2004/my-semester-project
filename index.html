Allai Valley is a mountainous region in Hazara Division, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. Experience the pristine meadows, sparkling streams, and majestic mountains that define its beauty.

History of Allai Valley

1595 - 1971

Allai Valley was an independent tribal state ruled by the Nawabs of Allai from the Bebal subsection of the Gabri Swati tribe. Maintained its own administrative system and currency.

Legendary Leadership

Nawab Arsala Khan Swati strongly resisted British colonial rule and led the Independent Swatis. Pokal served as the historical capital.

Swati Influence

The Gabri Swati subgroup and other Swati clans were influential in Hazara and surrounding areas. Known for their tribal structure and defense of their territories.

Cultural Diversity

People of Allai today include Swatis and Gujjars. Gujjar communities traditionally speak Hindko while also using Pashto, showing the valley's linguistic diversity.

2005 Earthquake

The devastating earthquake destroyed homes built of clay, wood, and mud. Reconstruction led to modern concrete structures.

District Status

Allai was officially declared a district on January 23, 2023. Current leadership includes Prince Muhammad Nawaz (MNA) and Engineer Zubair Khan (MPA).

Legacy

Allai Valley continues to be rich in tradition, tribal heritage, and natural beauty, with history tied to tribal rulership and resilience against disasters.
