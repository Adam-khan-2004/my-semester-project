Welcome to Allai Valley

Allai Valley is a breathtaking region located in Hazara Division, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. This website is created to promote the rich history, culture, and natural beauty of my homeland.

Valley Highlights

History of Allai Valley

Allai Valley was an independent tribal state from 1595 to 1971, ruled by the Nawabs of Allai from the Bebal subsection of the Gabri Swati tribe. The state maintained its own currency and administrative system.

Nawab Arsala Khan Swati is remembered as a legendary leader who strongly resisted British colonial rule and led the Independent Swatis. The historical capital of Allai was Pokal.

In 2005, a devastating earthquake struck Allai. Before the earthquake, houses were constructed mainly from clay, mud, and wood. After the disaster, the architecture completely transformed, and concrete structures became common across the valley.

Allai was officially declared a district on January 23, 2023. The current MNA is Prince Muhammad Nawaz, and the current MPA is Engineer Zubair Khan.

Culture & Lifestyle

Traditional Clothing

Men wear Shalwar Kameez with Pakol, while elders wear Parke. Women wear Shalwar Kameez with Burqa or Padhune. Clothing reflects Pakhtun values and mountain climate.

Values & Traditions

Respect for elders, joint family system, hospitality, and modesty are core values of Allai society.

Cuisine of Allai

Traditional food includes maize bread with Allai Saag, lassi, Gungri in winters, bean stews, rice flour bread, paneer, Ghunzakhi, Gulgule, and Weshala.
